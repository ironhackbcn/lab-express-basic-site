Interesting Facts About Swiss Mountains

The Alps are the highest and most extensive mountain range system that lies entirely in Europe, stretching approximately 1,200 kilometers (750 miles) across eight Alpine countries: Austria, France, Germany, Italy, Liechtenstein, Monaco, Slovenia, and Switzerland.

4 Interesting Facts!

  1. The Alps are the youngest and most densely populated mountain range in Europe. They were formed about 65 million years ago.

  2. The mean height of the mountain peaks is 2.5 kilometers (1.6 miles).

  3. The Alps maintain a permanent snow blanket over 2750 meters (9,000 feet) above sea level, and have many glaciers.

  4. The Alps are almost treeless near the top, but forests grow below. The higher forests have mostly spruce, pine, and larch trees. Oak, beech, and chestnut trees grow on the valley floors and lower slopes.
